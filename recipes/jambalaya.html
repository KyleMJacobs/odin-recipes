Jambalaya

Description

Jambalaya is a Cajun dish made of rice and some combination of meat and/or seafood. It's usually made in one pot.

Often compared or confused with Gumbo, Jambalaya is different from Gumbo in a few ways. Gumbo is more like a stew and served over just a bit of rice, wheras with Jambalaya the rice is cooked in the pot with everything else. Jambalaya is usually much thicker than Gumbo for this reason.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 8oz andouille sasage, cut into 1/4-inch slices
  • 2 tablespoons ground paprika
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 2 stalks of celery, sliced 1/4-inch thick
  • 1 large green bell pepper, diced
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup uncooked brown rice
  • 3 cups chicken stock
  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • salt and ground pepper to taste
